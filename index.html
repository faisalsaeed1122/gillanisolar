From 3 KV to 36 KV switchgear system is categorized as medium voltage switchgear. This switchgear is mostly metal enclosed indoor type. The interruption medium of this switchgear may be oil, SF and vacuum. The main requirement of MV power network is to interrupt current during faulty condition irrespective of what type of CB is used in the MV switchgear system.

Electrical switchgear rated up to 1 KV is termed as Low voltage switchgear. It includes low voltage circuit breakers, switches, off load electrical isolators, HRC fuses, earth leakage circuit breaker, miniature circuit breakers (MCB) and molded case circuit breakers (MCCB) and all other the accessories required to protect the LV system. The most common use of LV switchgear is in LV distribution board.

Secondary power systems i.e. generators are used in industries or commercial buildings and hospitals as emergency, prime or continuous power supply when utility power is lost. Some industries only require generator power for emergency lighting and other emergency systems but in Pakistan as the power failure issues or load shedding is common, the secondary power supply is mandatory. Facilities that have critical or life support systems utilize a more advanced distribution system.

AC/ DC Panels are used to supply power to auxiliary services in substation such as control/ automation system, monitoring system, lighting system, etc.   The AC/ DC Panels are equipped with Circuit Breakers for incoming and outgoing, Bus Bars Three Phase and Neutral for AC Panels, and Positive and Negative for DC Panels, Protective Devices such as AC No Voltage Relay, D.C Under Voltage Relay, Earth Fault Relays, Instruments, Meters, Annunciators, Etc.

Bus tie duct also known by the term BTD is a system of distributing electric power using copper busbars with suitable enclosures preferably metal structure to prevent the busducts from getting damaged from foreign bodies. Busbars are now becoming irreplaceable simply because of the convenience and safety factor. While using traditional cables, the cost of cabling and trunking and the time taken for installation is much higher than busbars.Comparision between using BTD and Cables.

view more

A distribution board is a component of an electricity supply system that divides power into different circuits and provides a protective fuse or circuit breaker for each circuit in a common enclosure. Distribution boards contain a main circuit breaker, which is the core part of these board panels. Its functionality is to split the total amount of electrical power (either single or three phase) and distributing it to mini circuit breakers present in the board enclosure.

view more

Local Control Cubical or Marshaling Kiosks (MKs) are specialized enclosures used in Substations, etc., that house electrical connections, control systems, and other equipment. Typically, MKs consists of Terminal Block, Circuit Breakers, Power Sockets, etc. These kiosks provide a safe and secure environment for electrical equipment, protecting it from environmental factors, such as weather, dust, and moisture.
